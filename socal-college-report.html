UC San Diego (UCSD)

Public research university · La Jolla, coastal · ~43,000 students

Best Known For

Computer ScienceEngineeringBiologyOceanographyData ScienceResearchPre-med

Admissions

  • Highly selective; GPA and course rigor weigh heavily
  • Uses UC Personal Insight Questions
  • Does not consider SAT/ACT
  • No recommendation letters for most applicants

Ideal Student

Someone interested in research, STEM, medicine, engineering, or graduate school.

Comparing Admissions Factors

Beyond GPA and testing, here's what each application actually weighs.

Essays — most important for

  1. USC
  2. UCSD
  3. USD
  4. LMU

Less significant for SDSU.

Extracurriculars — what matters

  1. Leadership & long-term commitment
  2. Community service, employment
  3. Athletics, clubs, research, personal projects

Depth of involvement generally matters more than the number of activities.

Standardized testing policy

  1. UCSD, SDSU — test blind (not considered)
  2. USD, USC, LMU — test optional

California Residency Considerations

If you intend to become a California resident after your first year

  • Tuition savings at UCSD and SDSU can be substantial — tens of thousands of dollars per year.
  • Simply living in California while attending college generally does not qualify you for in-state tuition.
  • Residency for tuition purposes typically requires evidence that California has become your permanent home for reasons other than education, plus meeting residency and financial-independence requirements.

Plan carefully if your financial strategy depends on qualifying for resident tuition.

Final Thoughts

This list provides an excellent balance of public and private institutions while offering strong programs across engineering, business, sciences, healthcare, and liberal arts.

What will determine admission success, in order of importance

  • GPA and course rigor
  • Quality of essays (where applicable)
  • Extracurricular depth and leadership
  • Intended major (especially for impacted programs)
  • Standardized test scores (only where they remain optional)
